# Rubrics

## Purpose

Use these rubrics when scoring learner output or producing assessment evidence. Scores should explain what changed in the plan, not just label performance.

## Common Dimensions

- `task_success`: did the answer satisfy the communicative task?
- `accuracy`: grammar, morphology, word order, spelling, and script control.
- `range`: vocabulary, structures, and discourse tools available.
- `naturalness`: idiom, collocation, register, and native-like phrasing for the level.
- `fluency`: speed, continuity, repair ability, and hesitation tolerance.
- `comprehension`: correct interpretation of input.

## Five-Point Band

Use for dimension scoring:

- `5`: clearly above target level.
- `4`: solid for target level; minor issues only.
- `3`: usable but uneven; needs targeted practice.
- `2`: partial control; frequent support needed.
- `1`: below level; task often breaks down.
- `0`: no usable evidence or failed task.

## Writing Rubric

Score:

- task success
- accuracy
- range
- coherence
- register/naturalness

Interpretation:

- `4-5 average`: raise complexity or start exam-format writing.
- `3 average`: keep level, target 1-2 recurring patterns.
- `0-2 average`: simplify prompt and build controlled production first.

Feedback must include corrected version, top issues, reusable patterns, and one revision task.

## Conversation Rubric

Score:

- task success
- intelligibility
- accuracy
- fluency
- repair ability
- register

Interpretation:

- Strong task success with low accuracy: continue fluency but add a grammar follow-up.
- Low task success: teach repair phrases and rerun the scenario.
- Good accuracy but low fluency: reduce correction and increase timed turns.

## Reading Rubric

Score:

- gist comprehension
- detail comprehension
- inference
- vocabulary-in-context
- summary/output

Interpretation:

- Gist high, detail low: train scanning and question-answer alignment.
- Vocabulary low: add selected SRS items, not every unknown word.
- Output low: route to writing or conversation with the source topic.

## Grammar Rubric

Score:

- recognition
- controlled production
- error repair
- free production
- transfer to mixed context

Interpretation:

- Recognition only: not learned; drill production.
- Controlled production good, free production weak: add contextual prompts.
- Transfer good: mark pattern as improving and reduce drill frequency.

## Vocabulary Rubric

Score or map each item:

- instant recall: SM-2 quality `5`
- correct with hesitation: `4`
- correct with effort or self-correction: `3`
- wrong but recognized after answer: `2`
- familiar but not recalled: `1`
- complete miss: `0`

Session-level interpretation:

- Many quality `0-2`: reduce new items and prioritize review.
- Mostly `3-5`: introduce new items or transfer tasks.
- Frequent same-confusion errors: create a weak pattern.

## Level Check Rubric

A level estimate needs evidence across:

- comprehension
- controlled production
- free production
- vocabulary range
- grammar control
- task success

Confidence:

- `high`: evidence from at least four dimensions including free production.
- `medium`: evidence from three dimensions, with at least one production task.
- `low`: fewer than three dimensions or no free production.

Only medium or high confidence should update `profile.languages.<language>.current_level`.
