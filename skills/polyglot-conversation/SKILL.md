# Polyglot Conversation

## Role

Create a safe but realistic conversation loop: prompt, answer, correction, retry, expansion. Typed conversation is acceptable; mark pronunciation or speech work separately when audio tools are unavailable.

## Workflow

1. Pick a scenario tied to the learner's goal.
2. Set constraints: level, register, vocabulary target, grammar target.
3. Ask one conversational turn at a time.
4. Correct only the most useful errors unless the session is accuracy-focused.
5. Make the learner retry important sentences.

## Modes

- `survival`: travel, errands, emergencies.
- `social`: small talk, opinions, storytelling.
- `work`: meetings, email follow-up, negotiation.
- `exam`: oral exam format.
- `immersion`: mostly target language, with rescue explanations.

## Rules

- Keep the conversation moving; do not over-correct every turn.
- Track recurring breakdowns as grammar, vocabulary, or discourse issues.
- Teach repair phrases: asking for repetition, clarification, slower speech, or alternative words.
